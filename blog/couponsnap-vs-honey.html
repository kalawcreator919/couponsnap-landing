The Honey Controversy Explained

In December 2024, multiple prominent YouTube creators — including MegaLag — published investigations claiming Honey was:

  • Replacing creators' affiliate tracking cookies with Honey's own, diverting commission payments away from the creators who referred the sales.
  • In some cases, showing intentionally lower-value coupon codes to maximize Honey's own affiliate earnings on high-margin product categories.
  • Collecting and monetizing browsing data across all websites, not just shopping sites.

PayPal/Honey disputed some of these claims but the reputational damage was significant. The Honey Chrome extension lost millions of users in a matter of weeks.
