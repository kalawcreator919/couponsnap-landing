Why People Are Leaving Honey

Honey's core product hasn't changed: it scans coupon databases and tries codes at checkout. But since the PayPal acquisition, users have flagged several concerns:

  • Cookie stuffing allegations — YouTubers and affiliate marketers reported Honey replacing their referral links, redirecting commissions away from content creators
  • Data monetization — Honey's privacy policy explicitly states that browsing and purchase data can be shared with PayPal's broader advertising network
  • Limited coupon coverage — Users report the extension often shows "no coupons found" even when valid codes are publicly available

None of this makes Honey inherently dangerous. But in 2026, you have better options — ones that are free, faster, and less invasive.

2. Capital One Shopping

Capital One Shopping (formerly Wikibuy) applies coupons automatically and also compares prices across retailers. It's free even if you don't have a Capital One card. The downside: it's more aggressive about nudging you toward partner retailers, and it requires an account.

3. Rakuten (formerly Ebates)

Rakuten gives you cashback rather than instant coupon codes. It's legitimate and pays out real money, but works differently from Honey — it's a rebate model, not a code-testing model. Great as a supplement, less useful for instant checkout savings.

4. RetailMeNot Genie

RetailMeNot's browser extension auto-applies promo codes from their database. Coverage is strong for US retailers. The extension is a bit heavier than CouponSnap and requires an account, but it's a solid free option with a long track record.

5. Coupert

Coupert is a newer entrant focused on Southeast Asian and European markets alongside the US. It also offers a cashback layer. Interface is clean. Coverage is growing but not yet at Honey or CouponSnap levels for US stores.

6. Karma

Karma helps you track price drops and reminds you about items you've wishlisted. It also applies coupons at checkout. It's more of a "shopping assistant" product than a pure coupon finder, which makes it useful for deliberate purchases but less impactful for impulse saves.

Does Stacking Extensions Work?

Yes — with caveats. CouponSnap and Honey can run simultaneously without breaking each other. The risk is that two extensions both try to apply codes at the same checkout moment, causing a popup clash. In practice this is rare.
